Chhabdi Bhat
______

The word "Chhabdi" means basket and "Bhat"
means "pattern" in Gujarati. The origins of this
pattern lie in an ancient fertility ritual for
to-be mothers. In this ritual a basket of flowers
would be placed on the woman's head.

Eventually, the basket was replaced by a cloth
with a motif of the basket of flowers. Since the
Patola cloth was highly valued and was believed
to sacred qualities, it came to be used as a
ritual object in this way.
Navratna Bhat
______

This pattern is known as the "Navratna Bhat".
"Navratna" means nine gems. The concept of
nine gems is ancient one in Indian mythology
Every gem is supposed to be associated with
a planet. A jewellery piece made with all the
nine gems is considered to be auspicious.
Later, this term was also used to describe
the 9 most extraordinary people or courtiers
in an emperor's court. Vikramaditya, Akbar
and Raja Krishnachandra were said to have
such nine "ratnas" in their courts.
